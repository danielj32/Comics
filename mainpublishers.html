Dc comics

DC Comics is an American comic book publisher. It is part of DC Entertainment, 1 of the companies that make up Warner Bros. Entertainment, which in turn is owned by WarnerMedia. It was founded in 1934 under the name National Allied Publications, to then take the name of DC Comics in 1937. The initials "DC" are an abbreviation of Detective Comics, one of the first emblematic titles of the company.

Marvel

Marvel Worldwide, Inc. is an American comic book publisher created in 1939, initially under the name of timely Publications. Among his emblematic characters of the superhero genre are Spider-man, Captain America, Iron Man, Wolverine, Hulk, Thor, Deadpool, the X-Men, the fantastic 4, the defenders and Inhumans, among others.

Darkhorse

Dark Horse Comics is an American comics and manga editor. It was founded in 1986 by Mike Richardson in Milwaukie, Oregon. Richardson began opening his first comic book store, Pegasus Books, in Bend, Oregon, in 1980. From there he was able to use the funds of his retail business to start his own editorial. Dark Horse Presents and Boris the Bear were the two initial titles in 1986 and, after a year of their first publication, Dark Horse Comics added nine new titles to their list, including Hellboy, the American, the Mask, Trekker and Black Cross. Frank Miller's Sin City is one of the most famous works associated with Dark horse, and has become a kind of signature comic for the publisher. They also established a reputation for publishing licensed works such as Aliens, Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Conan and Star Wars.
